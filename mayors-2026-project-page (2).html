Daily Maverick toured some of South Africa's worst municipalities. Here's what we found.

The investigation

How we reported this story — and what we discovered

Over six months, Daily Maverick journalists visited [X] municipalities across South Africa, combing through audit reports, interviewing residents, attending council meetings, and requesting access to infrastructure that officials would rather hide.

We chose our locations deliberately. Each municipality profiled here sits in the bottom quartile of the Auditor-General's outcomes, carries irregular expenditure running into hundreds of millions of rands, and faces local government elections in 2026.

“The pipes are broken, the roads are broken, and the promises are broken. But every five years they come back to
ask for your vote.”

Resident, Sundays River Valley, EC

Our methodology: reporters requested financial statements and audit outcomes under PAIA; verified service-delivery complaints against municipal records; and photographed conditions on the ground. All figures use the most recent audited data. Where officials were asked for comment, their responses are included in full.
